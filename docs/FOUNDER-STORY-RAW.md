And so we went to Hilscher. Originally as a firmware developer but as it is fate did not allow it. Due to the bug probation period in Solvay, the parameters changed and i have started with Python. And I liked it!!! My first project was to automated hardware resets of some boards the company was developing. So I wrote the tool and it worked. And then I had to refactor a framework for a gateway. And so I did and I liked it. And then I had to test a new MQTT client/server. But how to do it when most libraries are actually protected from too much hampering. And so the idea of a comm sdk was born that allows not only communication but an elaborate testing with crafted packets. And this naturally evolved to the emulator that mimicked all features of the product, even custom ones (e.g. custom packets or attributes). And so came OMB, DeviceNet, CAN and Modbus TLS. Many tools, many products tested, all automated tests. We had big customers from America for DeviceNet that were brutal and the milestones too. But we did the impossible and we delivered. The agile team of developer and tester working in parallel and delivering a TESTED solution at the end of the sprint was so successful that there was a decision to continue using this model for other projects too. And so I became a mentor, a teacher, developer and tester. I have trained 6 persons during this time with good feedback from te company. I was called a "master-blaster" and the "bastard from heaven".

But then a new transformation came and again freedom was lost and new bureaucracy introduced. And this time i knew it was time to leave. And so I started looking for a new challenge, a project that will allow me to continue developing new products with excellent solutions. And so came Heidelberg Meterials that bought a license of PxTrend from Paranext with the desire to make it better (refactor) for better security and usability. And so I thought, this is it. And again after 3 months, the story repeated itself. The nightmare came. Why? Because I landed again in a transformation, from our 3-5 year horizon i got 6 months guarantee. The creator of the product was disrespected and nobody made an effor to gathe rintelligence and facilitate the onboarding. Product owner didn't participate in the meetings for 4 months. The technical lead considered this project a source of free time and overly delegated stuff he didn't like to do. He didn't like anything, so he delegated evrything. At the end, 95% percent of his tasks were done by me, the rest AI. I was mistakenly assigned as an external which prevented me from accessing internal docs. It took 3 months to mitigate the issue. The TL didn't bother to provide access to running instances so that i can see what the product is. And so i took everything in my hands and i went to the nearest plant in Devnya (Cement). Then i spoke to the people, understood their problems and spoek about this with the creator of the product. Step by step my understanding started to expand. But the TL/PO were not happy. They didn't like that i spoke to our clients. They wanted we to have "peace". And so did our problems start. The TL is not accepting any other opinion but his. He cannot communicate with anytone. The creator of PxTrend, PO, etc. Each step is now a struggle. I fought to get user requests and incidents to work closely with the creator of PxTrend and learn the product. Things started to spread accross the plants but still i am critisized for my way. And this despite i help my team matees a lot, e.g. Holger with data integration from plants following non-conformant tag scheme that i solved with Edgar (the creator). Peter with the security reports and code analysis. And Nora, our devops with the test of the artifacts.

Somehow after 12 months, despite all the effort and results, i still did not receive the recognition i deserved. And so i decided to start my own company. And here we are. After the birth of my daughter, i decided it is time to change.

But then came the change. Solvay decided to transorm. And transform it was. From direct communication with other departments to heavy procedures and bureaucracy delaying and stopping everything. Engineers residing on two floors in the same building were now separated by kilometers. communication dropped. Then other transformations came to optimize the budgets and thus requiring 2-3-5 signatures even for small amoutns. Then management changed and they came with their own new toolign and thus double and tripple work from one sheet into another one. And it was time for change.

Automating with Siemens, porting Honeywell libraries to Siemens. The feeling after seeing a running installation aftert the hard work is incredible.

Cleaning up the mess of consultants, that broke the control database and left exposing humans at risk and equipment damage because these IT guys do not read their own manuals which updates to apply.

I started working as an automation engineer and i liked it a lot. I had a lot of adventures while working in Solvay Sodi, Devnya. Like flloding in the brine sector, shuffling water with bare hands so that no water reaches the PLCs. Storms that broke the electricity and stopped the lines moving vagonettes with limestone that we had to inspect with drones. Climbing 180m chimneys in windy conditions. Observing the how operators and field engineers test the draft of cooling towers by pissing and observing how it reacts. Observing field technicians test products by freezing them deeply and then throwing them from the second floor to simulate operator handling in the winter when the equipment freezes. Beeign gassed by ammonia thus having the life go in front of your eyes like in the movies. Working with soapy hands in the dense soda department with radioactive equipment in the christmas night. Observing how a steam head exploded through a meter thick concrete and this understanding the power of steam. Having fought physically with external personal because stupid management made us take pictures of them to observe safety. Chilling at night with operators during the commisioning of new PLCs and control logic. Joking about pissing on a cabinet that predates the internet era and works only with DOS. Short-cirtuiting a cabinet supply and thus stopping the production because of faulty design by Honeywell. Crawling thrugh narrow corridors and tunnels. Being called "Bhai" as a reward much better than having a Mater or PhD. Knowing that people will relax and say "If Branko is here, then eveyrthing will be all right". Team work with other colleagues when things go bad.
